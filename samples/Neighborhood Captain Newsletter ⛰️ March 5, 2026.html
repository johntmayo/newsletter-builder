Recovery Updates

  • Kathryn Barger secured a motion to expand temporary housing options. This is intended to help survivors who have site constraints preventing them from placing mobile units or RVs on their own lots during the rebuilding process.

  • LA County Approves $3.8M to Help Fire Survivors Add ADUs. More info will be released soon, but you can read about it here.

  • SBA representatives are available in-person for those who have already applied and require additional guidance to complete or move their application forward. On a case-by-case basis, new applications may be accepted due to extraordinary circumstances.

    • Walk-ins welcome or schedule an appointment.

    • The Collaboratory (540 W Woodbury Rd.), Monday-Friday @ 9-6 pm

    • 800-659-2955, Monday-Friday @ 8-8 pm ET

  • Tax season is coming up! EFSN hosted a MUST WATCH video on the essential things to know when preparing your taxes after a total loss. A follow up is happening today (March 5th). Details in the events section below.


  • To date, 1,669 building permits have been issued and 13 homes have been rebuilt in and around Altadena, according to Los Angeles County’s permitting progress dashboard.


  • LA County Infrastructure Survey: Help prioritize the next decade of funding for Altadena’s roads, water systems, and flood control by joining the West San Gabriel Valley CIP survey. Take the Survey

  • EFRU has partnered with Assemblymember Harabedian on AB 1642, an indoor testing bill that would establish health-based thresholds for indoor contamination and clear, science-driven protocols for post-WUI testing.

  • EFRU & LA Standing Homes: EFRU and LA Standing Homes are collecting signatures and policyholder accounts to identify insurer-specific patterns. Letters will be sent to the Los Angeles County Office of the Deputy County Counsel requesting investigations into potential bad-faith claims handling impacting standing homes.

    • Sign the letter to Mercury, Farmers, Allstate, Safeco/Liberty Mutual, AAA, CA FAIR Plan

  • A proposed class action was filed on March 4, 2026 in L.A. County Superior Court against Farmers Insurance. The suit alleges the company used "unreliable vendors" and "flawed testing" to systematically deny smoke and ash damage claims in Altadena and Pasadena.

  • A new bill, SB 1417, requires disaster-impacted water systems to conduct comprehensive resilience assessments — including potential consolidation reviews — as a prerequisite for receiving state recovery funding.

  • The Community Wildfire Protection Ordinance is now available for 30-day public review before being brought to the Regional Planning Commission for public hearing on Wednesday, March 25, 2026.

  • The Los Angeles County Sheriff’s Department Altadena Station has a free Construction Check Program, providing regular courtesy checks to help prevent theft, vandalism, and trespassing during the rebuilding process.
    • Email altadenahomecheck@lasd.org with:

      • Your name, address, and a valid callback number

      • Include construction company, phone number, and whether construction is active


Upcoming Deadlines

  • LA County Emergency Rent/Mortgage Relief Program Round 2 | DEADLINE March 11, 2026 (5 pm)
    The portal now allows tenants to initiate the process themselves, rather than relying solely on landlord-initiated application, but landlords must complete their sections.
  • The CalAssist Mortgage Fund Reopened and provides up to $100,000 in non-repayable mortgage assistance, covering up to 12 months of payments. Now features higher income limits (up to $281,400 in LA County) to help more disaster survivors qualify. Applicants are selected on a first come first serve basis.
  • Union Station Housing Assistance
    Direct financial aid for Eaton Fire survivors covering security deposits, rent, motel vouchers, and essential furnishings.
  • Bill & Linda James Scholarship | DEADLINE March 31, 2026
    ART has developed the Bill and Linda James Scholarship Fund to support young scholars heading off to college or currently pursuing higher education. $1,000 minimum awards
  • The Step Up Fund is giving $2,500 to fire survivors who face eviction | APPL
  • Helen Morran-Wolf Educational Scholarship | DEADLINE April 1
    Through Foothill Family, ten students pursuing first-time higher education (Bachelors) or vocational training will receive up to $5,000
  • Pacific Community Ventures’ RESTORE LA Fund for Micro & Small Business Recovery & Climate Resilience is a $3 million fund offering 3%, no-fee loans of up to $100,000 to small businesses who have experienced revenue declines due to the 2025 LA wildfires.
    SoCal Edison Wildfire Recovery Compensation Program | DEADLINE November 30, 2026
