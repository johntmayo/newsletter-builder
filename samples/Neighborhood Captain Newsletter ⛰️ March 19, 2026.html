Recovery Updates

  • MILESTONE ALERT! This week Regional Planning is expected to approve new zoning applications for over half of the structures destroyed in the Eaton fire, an incredible accomplishment and sign of resilience! To date, 1,978 building permits have been issued and 26 homes have been rebuilt in and around Altadena, according to Los Angeles County’s permitting progress dashboard.

  • LA County Capital Improvement Plan: Help prioritize the next decade of funding for Altadena’s roads, water systems, and flood control.

    • Kickoff Zoom: April 8 @ 6 pm – Register

    • Take the Survey

  • Watch the Altadena Town Council meeting from March 17, including discussion of the county’s consideration of RV street parking permits, and a presentation by SCE followed by public comment.

  • Protect yourself against fraud by signing up for the LA County Enhanced Homeowners Notification Program, which sends you copies of documents recorded against your home. This gives you a chance to review the real estate documents to be sure they are legitimate. It also gives you your best chance to save your home from foreclosure.

  • LA County Post-Fire Environmental Dashboard: New environmental assessment results available from CAP.LA, EFRU, and CSUN.

    • Scroll through results monitoring surface soils, coastal sand, and ash in wildfire-affected regions to assess the extent of contamination and potential exposure, which can inform comparisons to benchmarks and guide public health recommendations.

    • LA County Department of Public Health results show that of the 1,321 soil lead samples from Altadena, 55% were less than 80 ppm (Calfironia soil lead screening level), 37% were 81-200ppm (federal guidance threshold), 8% were 201-1000ppm, and 1 sample was greater than 1000ppm (hazardous waste threshold).

  • SCE announced executive bonuses after the Eaton Fire, but increased their total pay due increased company profits ($1.3 billion in 2024 to $4.5 billion in 2025) from wildfire damage protections and a 13% hike in electricity rates.

  • Tax season is coming up! Watch EFSN’s webinars on preparing your taxes. Always consult your tax professional about any information received.

    • Part 1: Total Loss

    • Part 2: Total Loss, Small Business, Condo Owners, Tenants, Standing Homes (See comments for timestamps.)

  • Senator Perez introduced a new bill, SB 1076, that would require home insurance companies to offer coverage to owners who complete wildfire-risk reduction steps.

  • Explore interactive maps of rebuilding with the LA County immersive GIS visualizations


  • LA County passed a motion to accept $5 million in state CalHome Program funds to support eligible households to receive down payment assistance of up to $200,000 or 40% of the purchase price.

  • Insurance Commissioner Ricardo Lara is sponsoring three pieces of legislation aimed at stabilizing the insurance market and providing relief to homeowners following the 2025 wildfires. Click to generate letters to your representatives:

    • AB 1680 (Make It FAIR Act)

    • SB 876 (Disaster Recovery Reform Act)

    • AB 1795 (Smoke Damage Recovery Act)

  • EFRU has partnered with Assemblymember Harabedian on AB 1642, an indoor testing bill that would establish health-based thresholds for indoor contamination and clear, science-driven protocols for post-WUI testing.

  • EFRU & LA Standing Homes: EFRU and LA Standing Homes are collecting signatures and policyholder accounts to identify insurer-specific patterns. Letters will be sent to the Los Angeles County Office of the Deputy County Counsel requesting investigations into potential bad-faith claims handling impacting standing homes.

    • Sign the letter to Mercury, Farmers, Allstate, Safeco/Liberty Mutual, AAA, CA FAIR Plan


Upcoming Deadlines

Bill & Linda James Scholarship | DEADLINE March 31, 2026
ART has developed the Bill and Linda James Scholarship Fund to support young scholars heading off to college or currently pursuing higher education. $1,000 minimum awards.

Helen Morran-Wolf Educational Scholarship | DEADLINE April 1

Through Foothill Family, ten students pursuing first-time higher education (Bachelors) or vocational training will receive up to $5,000.

LA County Emergency Rent/Mortgage Relief Program
Round 2 closed on March 11. Complete the Interest Form to receive more information. The portal now allows tenants to initiate the process themselves. Landlords must still complete their sections.

The CalAssist Mortgage Fund Reopened and provides up to $100,000 in non-repayable mortgage assistance, covering up to 12 months of payments. Now features higher income limits (up to $281,400 in LA County).

Union Station Housing Assistance
Direct financial aid for Eaton Fire survivors covering security deposits, rent, motel vouchers, and essential furnishings.

The Step Up Fund is giving $2,500 to fire survivors who face eviction.

Pacific Community Ventures’ RESTORE LA Fund for Micro & Small Business Recovery & Climate Resilience is a $3 million fund offering 3%, no-fee loans of up to $100,000 to small businesses who have experienced revenue declines due to the 2025 LA wildfires.


SoCal Edison Wildfire Recovery Compensation Program | DEADLINE November 30, 2026
