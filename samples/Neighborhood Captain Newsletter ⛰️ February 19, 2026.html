Recovery Updates

  • Residents organize to block SCE undergrounding that could threaten Altadena’s remaining mature trees.


  • Yesterday, SCE announced updating their compensation protocols to increase attorney fee coverage to 20% of net damages and additional rental support for tenants to include 3 months at the higher of actual pre-fire rental rates or monthly fair rental value.

    • SCE press release (February 18, 2026)

    • Also: LA County Prosecutors Probe Edison for Criminal Liability


  • Las Flores Water Company Update

    • The Board of Las Flores voted unanimously on February 17th to approve the “Fire Recovery Assessment", a $3,000 per share holder fee (billed as $50 month for 5 years)

  • Lincoln Water Avenue Company Update

    • Water rates will increase 18% (10% previously approved for 2025, but not implemented, and 8% for 2026)

    • Standby fees based on meter size will be reinstated in 2026 to cover costs to maintain pipes, regardless of meter activity. These are normal service fees that were waived in 2025.

    • The board considered, but voted not to implement a $15/month temporary recovery fee. They will reassess their financial condition in 6 months.

    • More info on these changes can be found in their notification.


  • Review the Extended Construction Hours survey results and the new county variance for construction noise in Altadena.

    • For any noise issues or complaints visit this link.

  • Attorney General Bonta Announces Investigation into Eaton Fire Emergency Response in West Altadena

    • Read the Press Release

  • SBA representatives are available in-person for those who have already applied and require additional guidance to complete or move their application forward. On a case-by-case basis, new applications may be accepted due to extraordinary circumstances.
    • Walk-ins welcome or schedule an appointment.

      • The Collaboratory (540 W Woodbury Rd.) Monday-Friday @ 9-6 pm

    • Phone: 800-659-2955 from Monday-Friday @ 8-8 pm ET

  • Watch the February 17th Altadena Town Council Meeting

  • The CalAssist Mortgage Fund Reopened

    • Provides up to $100,000 in non-repayable mortgage assistance, covering up to 12 months of payments.

    • Now features higher income limits (up to $281,400 in LA County) to help more disaster survivors qualify.

    • Applicants are selected on a first come first serve basis.

  • EFRU has partnered with Assemblymember Harabedian on AB 1642 — an indoor testing bill that would establish health-based thresholds for indoor contamination and clear, science-driven protocols for post-WUI testing.

  • EFRU & LA Standing Homes: Join the effort to document and report insurer bad-faith practices affecting standing-home survivors. EFRU and LA Standing Homes are collecting signatures and policyholder accounts to identify insurer-specific patterns. Letters will be sent to the Los Angeles County Office of the Deputy County Counsel requesting investigations into potential bad-faith claims handling impacting standing homes.

    • Sign the letter to Mercury, Farmers, Allstate, Safeco/Liberty Mutual, AAA, CA FAIR Plan

  • Read the CAP.LA report or watch the recording on soil testing after the Eaton and Palisades fires.

  • Unlock Funding to Rebuild Safe, All-Electric Homes by urging Governor Newsom to authorize the CPUC-approved RISE Homes program. This will help secure financial incentives to rebuild all-electric, climate-resilient homes that lower costs, improve health, cut emissions, and help families rebuild stronger. Sign these petitions:

    • Stop Ratepayer-Funded Gas Incentives and Accelerate Safe, Affordable Electric Recovery After the 2025 Wildfires

    • Urge Governor Newsom to implement RISE Homes program

  • The Community Wildfire Protection Ordinance is now available for 30-day public review before being brought to the Regional Planning Commission for public hearing on Wednesday, March 25, 2026.

  • The Los Angeles County Sheriff’s Department Altadena Station has a free Construction Check Program, to provide regular courtesy checks to help prevent theft, vandalism, and trespassing during the rebuilding process.

    • Email: altadenahomecheck@lasd.org with your name, address, and a valid callback number. Please include whether construction is active, as well as the construction company name and phone number.

Upcoming Deadlines

Round 2 of LA County’s Emergency Rent/Mortgage Relief Program. Deadline is Wednesday, March 11, 2026 at 4:59 PM. The portal now allows tenants to initiate the process themselves, rather than relying solely on landlord-initiated application, but landlords must complete their sections.

Bill & Linda James Scholarship - $1,000 Minimum Award
ART has developed the Bill and Linda James Scholarship Fund to support young scholars heading off to college or currently pursuing higher education. Deadline March 31st.


The Step Up Fund is giving $2,500 to fire survivors who face eviction. Apply online.

The Daz-E Shop’s Suzanne Burger scholarships will be granted to ten Altadena women whose educations were impacted by the Eaton Fire. Applications must be postmarked no later than February 28. Call 626-794-6244.


Pacific Community Ventures’ RESTORE LA Fund for Micro & Small Business Recovery & Climate Resilience is a $3 million fund offering 3%, no-fee loans of up to $100,000 to small businesses who have experienced revenue declines due to the 2025 LA wildfires.
