Recovery Updates

  • LA County Capital Improvement Plan: Help prioritize the next decade of funding for Altadena’s roads, water systems, and flood control.

  • San Gabriel Valley Habitat for Humanity completed its first fire-rebuild in Altadena utilizing pre-approved designs from the Foothill Catalog Foundation. Habitat is slated to build an additional 21 houses with funding from Altadena Builds Back Foundation.

  • Protect yourself against fraud by signing up for the LA County Enhanced Homeowners Notification Program, which sends you copies of documents recorded against your home. This gives you a chance to review the real estate documents to be sure they are legitimate. It also gives you your best chance to save your home from foreclosure.

  • LA County Post-Fire Environmental Dashboard: New environmental assessment results available from CAP.LA, EFRU, and CSUN.

    • Scroll through results monitoring surface soils, coastal sand, and ash in wildfire-affected regions to assess the extent of contamination and potential exposure, which can inform comparisons to benchmarks and guide public health recommendations.

    • LA County Department of Public Health results show that of the 1,321 soil lead samples from Altadena, 55% were less than 80 ppm (Calfironia soil lead screening level), 37% were 81-200ppm (federal guidance threshold), 8% were 201-1000ppm, and 1 sample was greater than 1000ppm (hazardous waste threshold).

  • Tax season is coming up! Watch EFSN’s webinars on preparing your taxes. Always consult your tax professional about any information received.

    • Part 1: Total Loss

    • Part 2: Total Loss, Small Business, Condo Owners, Tenants, Standing Homes (See comments for timestamps.)

  • The Dept of Angels is currently fielding their next survey to capture the current needs of LA survivors at the 15-month mark. If you or your neighbors receive a text message, please encourage them to complete the brief survey.

  • EFRU has partnered with Assemblymember Harabedian on AB 1642, an indoor testing bill that would establish health-based thresholds for indoor contamination and clear, science-driven protocols for post-WUI testing.

      • Clean Power Alliance (Sun Storage Rebate)

      FAFSA Professional Judgement

      College-bound students who submitted a FAFSA should request a Professional Judgement directly from each school’s financial aid office. Administrators can adjust packages based on special circumstances like disasters. (Insurance payouts are counted as liquid assets, reducing aid.) Make the request before any decisions are reached; each school's process is unique and their decisions are final.
